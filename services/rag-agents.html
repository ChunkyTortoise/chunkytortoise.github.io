Why Generic Chatbots Fail

Off-the-shelf AI assistants aren't built for your documents. They're built for general knowledge, which is exactly what you don't need.

×

Hallucination with confidence

Generic chatbots answer questions they don't know the answer to. RAG anchors every response to passages from your actual documents. No source, no answer.

×

No source citations

When the answer is wrong, there's no way to trace why. Cited answers let you verify the output, identify gaps in your documents, and improve over time.

×

Can't handle your domain

Pretrained knowledge doesn't include your product docs, internal policies, contracts, or pricing. A RAG agent knows exactly what you feed it, and nothing else.

What Gets Built

A production pipeline, not a prototype. Every component is tested, documented, and ready to hand off.

1

Document Ingestion

PDFs, Word docs, web pages, databases, and APIs. Type-aware parsing handles each format correctly.

2

Type-Aware Chunking

Different chunking strategies for invoices vs. contracts vs. manuals. Structure is preserved, not discarded.

3

Hybrid Embedding

BM25 keyword search and pgvector dense embeddings run in parallel. Keyword precision plus semantic recall.

4

Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF)

Combines both retrieval methods to rank results by agreement. Better than either approach alone.

5

Citation-Aware Generation

Claude generates answers that cite the specific source passages used. Every claim is traceable.

6

Fallback Routing

Complex or low-confidence questions are flagged for human review with full context attached. No silent failures.
