The Problem with AI Acquisitions

AI is the fastest-moving category in M&A right now. It's also where due diligence most often fails. These are the risks that don't show up in the pitch deck.

⚠

"Proprietary AI" is just API wrappers

Claimed in-house AI turns out to be thin wrappers around OpenAI or Claude with no proprietary value. The IP you're acquiring doesn't exist.

⚠

Hidden infrastructure costs

$5,000/month API bills buried in the technical details. Scaling to 10x users means $50,000/month. That changes the unit economics of the entire deal.

⚠

Technical debt that kills the acquisition

Models not versioned. No retraining pipeline. Manual prompt editing in production. Rebuild cost: $500,000+. This shows up in post-close, not before.

⚠

Compliance landmines

Patient data processed without HIPAA compliance. Customer data handled without GDPR documentation. Regulatory risk is real liability, and it's unpriced in the deal.
